The standard time above is computed by getting the average of the normal time given in each cycle multiplied by the appropriate allowances (personal interruptions, fatigue, and unavoidable delays).
ST = (NT)(1 + Allowances)
This is important so that a company knows how much time an average operator, working at a normal pace, can perform a specified task. The company can then plan accordingly with that knowledge in mind.
Normal Time
NT = (OT)(R)
The normal time (per cycle) is found by adding the observed time of the elements in a cycle and multiplying this by the performance rating of that cycle. We use NT to find the standard time as seen above.
