time study

This is a work measurement technique done by slicing the task into elements and timing each element, establishing ratings, and assigning appropriate allowances to determine time standards. It is mostly used for routine tasks.

In Industrial Engineering, it is part of Methods Engineering, which deals with constructing new methods from existing ones. Methods Engineering is one of the four ways of generating solutions in a systems study. Others are Production Systems, Human Factors and Ergonomics, and Operations Research.

history & importance

The time (and motion) study used today is a combination of the motion study invented by Frank B. Gilbreth and Lillian M. Gilbreth and the time study invented by Frederick W. Taylor in 1881.

“Time study is the one element in scientific management beyond all others making possible the transfer of skill from management to men.” - Frederick W. Taylor

Time studies are important as they assist a company in evaluating and analyzing costs and productivity. They highlight where a facility needs to improve in order to cut costs, and improve efficiencies. It enhances production quality and utilizes maximum resource capacity in minimum time.

conducting a time study

In this site, the following steps were followed (with some changes to the ordering) in order to conduct an accurate time study.

  1. Break down the task into elements
  2. Record the observed time per element
  3. Assign performance rating per element or per cycle
  4. Calculate the normal time
  5. Provide appropriate allowances
  6. Compute for standard time
